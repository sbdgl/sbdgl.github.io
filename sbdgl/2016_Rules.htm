2016 Sutliff Bushwhacker Disc Golf League Rules


  1. League play is intended for fun, the love of the game and friendly competition, respectively.
  2. Entry Fee: $80.00/person. Dues must be paid on sign up night (4/6/16). NO PAY- NO PLAY!
  3. Time: Wednesdays, starting by 6:00 PM. Please be courteous to ALL other players and park users. Please be early or on time. If you can't be on time, please contact your partner or opponents and don't begin on a hole that may cause slower play by other groups.
  4. Format: Each week partners will play opponents head to head for 3 pts., best avg. vs. best avg. and 2nd best avg. vs. 2nd best avg. plus/minus handicaps for 1 point each. A third point will be awarded to the team with the lowest cumulative score after handicap. Ties will result in 1/2 point awarded to each player and/or team. Please keep groups to a max of 4 players.
  5. Draft Night: After sign up night the Rules Committee will meet to randomly choose draft position for each player. Players draw for their partner in order of draft position at Parlor City Bar on Wednesday, April 13th, beginning at 6 pm. Players will have 5 minutes from their draft time to draw. If a player is unable to draw within the given time frame, a Rules Committee member will draw for them. Ask Woody if any questions.
  6. Handicap: We will use an 80% handicap based off of a score of 46. Handicaps will be set by last year's handicap for the first round. The second round handicap will be set by the average of last year's handicap and the first round. Subsequent rounds will be based on your average for all rounds this year. New players and subs will need two weeks to set a handicap; however, a subs first round will be counted scratch.
  7. Cancellations: Plan to play no matter what! The Rules Committee will have the final say on cancelling a league event. If cancelled, the rules committee will schedule a make-up round. If weather is going to occur during the round, the rules committee will decide an appropriate and safe course of action.
  8. Absences: If you are unable to play on Wednesday night, you should:
    1. Contact your partner
    2. Make-up the round at 10 AM the following Sunday at the same venue
    3. Find a sub
    If no sub plays on Wednesday, and you don't make up the round on Sunday, YOU WILL FORFIET YOUR POINTS! If you miss league TWICE (no call/no show), you may be removed from your team at the discretion of the Rules Committee. If your partner drops for any reason, the Rules Committee will pair you with the next most established sub.
  9. Subs: You or your partner must be responsible for finding/choosing a sub and making sure a league administrator knows of the substitution. Subs must pay $5.00 at some point on league night for their round to be scored. Subs may become members of league at a prorated cost making them eligible for prizes, ace fund, party, etc. Extra subs must play on a sub card. No groups larger than 4 players.
  10. Church/Sunday Make-up: There will be a limit of 4 Church rounds per player. Extra Church rounds will be dealt with on a case by case basis by the Rules Committee. Church starts promptly at 10 AM. At 10:15 if you're not there, you will forfeit your points and no score will be counted. Subs cannot play for an individual or team score on Sunday, however, they can pay to have their score kept and handicap figured. Players who miss Wednesday and got a Sub can play Church for yearly/career stats but no points. If a conflict arises (e.g. a tournament takes over the course on Sunday and the round can't be played or you're playing in a tournament at the make-up course) you may make arrangements to make up the round with Rules Committee approval.
  11. Honors/Tees/Alternate Tees: The score card will dictate order for the first hole. Honors, group discretion, or speed of play may all be used during the round. We play all long tees at every course when possible. At the group's consensus, if the original tee is unsafe or unusable, use of an alternate tee is permitted as long as it's no closer to the hole.
  12. Finishing a Hole: The disc must be at rest in the basket or hanging in the chains to be completed. Resting on top or hanging from any exterior part of the basket doesn't count as completed. Any wedged disc will count as long as it is retrieved from the basket before falling out. “Gimme” putts may be offered by opposing team members only. ALWAYS PLAN TO PUTT OUT!
  13. Out of Bounds: There will be no out of bounds. If stuck in a tree, play from beneath it. There will be no taking a stroke to get out of a jam, play from where it lies. If in water, refer to rule 15B.
  14. Scoring: Members are required to pay attention to their opponents score hole by hole. Disputes will be settled by the group's consensus, OR refer to rule 15D.
  15. Penalty Strokes: All stroke penalties will be given at the group's consensus.
    1. Lost Disc – One stroke penalty. There will be a max of 5 minutes, timed by the group, to search for a lost disc. Please reference the notable course etiquette. Be aware of your group's throws and spot if necessary. Play will resume from as close to the previous spot as possible. If the disc is lost off the tee, re-tee.
    2. Water Hazard – If you throw into water, you can:
      1. Play from your lie in the water for no penalty
      2. Play from the point of entry into the water for a one stroke penalty
      3. Play from the previous throws lie for a one stroke penalty
    3. Mismarking Lie – One stroke penalty. A warning shall be given. Players must mark their lie either by using a marker, or by leaving a thrown disc where it rests. Flipping of discs will be allowed only if no advantage is gained. If warned, instruction on how to flip properly shall be given. Also throwing, or changing the lie of another players thrown disc for purposes other than identification.
    4. Stroke Shaving/Padding – One stroke penalty. A warning shall be given. Serious cases will be dealt with by the Rules Committee. Padding cases will require a Rules Committee investigation resulting in possible dismissal from league.
    5. Falling Putt – Players must show control behind their lie within 10 meters (32.8ft). If a violation is called, a warning and instruction on how to putt correctly shall be given. No re-putt necessary. Each offense after, however, will result in a one stroke penalty.
    6. 2nd Throws/Practice Putts – One stroke penalty. A warning shall be given first. If you want to take an 2nd throw or practice putt you must have your opponent's approval first to not be penalized.
    7. Breaking any League Rule – One stroke penalty. A warning shall be given first.
  16. Hobo Rule: If any no league person or animal picks up, moves, and/or affects your lie get group consensus on a new lie as close as possible to the original. No penalty shall be assessed.
  17. Tie Team Records at End of Season: In the event of a tie, team points will be the tiebreaker. If that were to be tied too, team average will be the next tie breaker. If you're not happy with your team average... Fucking shoot better!
  18. Rules Committee: At the conclusion of the 2016 season, all members in attendance at the last night of league will vote on the five members of the 2017 rules committee with the highest vote getter being President. (Votes are for one person, one time.)
  19. Pets/"Distractions": No pets allowed. Please be courteous to other league members and leave your pets at home. Be courteous and limit any extra “third party” distractions.
  20. League Party/Supplies: The league party is intended to celebrate the end of the league season and includes at no extra cost: food, beverage, contests and games, and awards ceremony, just bring yourself and have a great time! Please drink responsibly and get a ride home if it is necessary. Any supplies needed for the operation of the SBDGL shall be paid with league funds.

    21. Notable Course Etiquette:
    Please abide by all Park, State, and Federal laws
    Heckle freely, but be courteous to each other while in putting/throwing motion
    Speed of play; please allow faster groups to play through (especially in cases of a lost disc)
    Pick up trash and respect the course/park and all public users


    Prizes/Awards: Payouts will be based on member numbers and attendance for individual awards. Subs refer to rule 9.
    • Cash Payouts - Top placing teams - Trophies for 1st-3rd
    • Division Champions - Coolers with 6 pack of your choice
    • MVP - Most Individual Points - In case of a tie, lowest average will be the tiebreaker - Trophy
    • Best Average - Player with the lowest average through 18 weeks - Trophy
    • Bushwhacker Award - More than just Golf - Trophy
    • Machete Award - Most improved - Machete
    • Rookie of the Year - First year player with the most individual points - Trophy
    • Ace Fund/Beer/Bounty - Money and 6 pack of your choice - Ace with a current year Bushwhacker league disc and win more money
    • Course Record - Break any course record and receive a 6 pack of your choice
    • 100/200 Round Tags - Players who play 100 or 200 league rounds receive a commemorative bag tag
    • Belt - Player who scores the lowest scratch round each week
    • CTP - Closest to Pin - All members play a designated hole each week, the disc that lands closest gets a prize
    • Bag Tags - Lowest score on the card after handicap gets the lowest tag - tags not in play will forfeit tag for next week
    • Lord of the Whackers - Lowest score after handicap out of the entire league each week - Boot Trophy

Bushwhacker Cup Match Play Tournament


At the conclusion of the regular Bushwhacker league season there will be a single elimination, seeded, individual match play tournament where players will play head to head with their handicap.

Seeds will be based off of an individual's total points earned during the regular league season. If there were to be multiple players with the same amount of total points, their year's average will be the tie breaker with the lower average getting the higher seed. Seed numbers will be dictated by attendance. An example of seeds would be 1-16, with 1 being the highest and 16 being lowest.

In the event where there’s three on a card, elimination rules will take into effect, there will be no byes into future rounds. You must play to be able to advance.

Entry fee is $5.00 and included in league dues with 100% payout to the top 3 to 4 players.

All league rules will apply. Handicap will adjust accordingly.

Rounds will be each Wednesday following league and plan to start as close to 5:30 pm as daylight is an issue this late in the year.

If you win each match the tournament should only take 4-5 weeks depending on format.

The Champion gets his/her name on the Bushwhacker Cup trophy and their choice of drink which they will consume from the cup at the following years league party.

The Great Whack Off: Sutliff vs. Des Moines Bushwhackers


The Great Whack Off is an annual competition with our fellow Bushwhackers from Des Moines. It is a league formatted tournament and the cost is $10.00. All current league members are encouraged to play. Location is determined by the winning team from the previous year.
